# Working Brief — Australian Economic Outlook (12-Month)

*Initialised: 2026-02-23*

---

## Thesis Direction

Australia faces a unique late-cycle challenge: the RBA reversed its easing cycle with a hike to 3.85% in Feb 2026 as inflation re-accelerated (CPI 3.8%, trimmed mean 3.4%), yet the labour market remains tight (4.1% unemployment) and growth is recovering toward trend (~2.1%). This is a stagflationary undercurrent — not classic stagflation, but a tension between sticky inflation above target and below-trend per-capita growth. The policy-rate U-turn is the defining feature: markets price further hikes (76% chance of May hike), creating asymmetric risk for rate-sensitive assets (housing, consumer discretionary, long-duration equities). The commodity mix provides a partial offset — gold surging, copper strong, iron ore declining but still dominant. AUD benefiting from rate differential widening vs USD.

**Investment clock position:** Late-cycle, inflation-persistent quadrant. Growth recovering but constrained by policy tightening.

## Key Data Points

- RBA cash rate: 3.85% (hiked 25bp on 3 Feb 2026, first hike since Nov 2023) → Hawkish pivot; markets price 76% chance of May hike to 4.10%
- CPI: 3.8% YoY to Dec 2025 (ABS, 28 Jan 2026) → Well above 2-3% target band
- Trimmed mean: 3.4% YoY to Dec 2025 (ABS) → RBA forecasts peak at 3.7% mid-2026 before easing to 2.6% by mid-2028
- Unemployment: 4.1% (Jan 2026, ABS 19 Feb 2026) → Below consensus 4.2%, below RBA's 4.5% estimate of NAIRU
- Employment: +17,800 Jan, full-time +50,500 to record 10.16M → Labour market stubbornly tight
- WPI: 3.4% YoY Q4 2025 (ABS) → Wages running above level consistent with 2% inflation
- GDP: 0.4% QoQ Sep 2025, IMF projects 1.8% 2025, 2.1% 2026 → Below trend per capita
- GDP per capita: Negative for 9 of last 11 quarters → Living standards declining
- Consumer confidence: Westpac 92.9 (Jan 2026), below neutral 100 → Consumers wary
- ASX 200: ~9,060, fwd P/E 20.9x (20-year avg 15.9x) → Elevated valuations
- AU 10Y yield: 4.74% → Elevated, reflecting hawkish RBA and fiscal concerns
- AUD/USD: 0.7050-0.7075, up 5.5% YTD → Second best G10 performer in 2026
- Household debt: 189% of income → Record high, among highest globally
- Mortgage stress: 24.5% "at risk" (Dec 2025), lowest since Jan 2023 but set to rise with hikes
- Budget deficit: A$36.8B in 2025-26 (MYEFO), net debt rising from 32% to 35.7% of GDP by 2027-28
- Iron ore: US$87/t 2025, forecast US$85/t 2026 → Declining on African supply + China diversification
- Gold: Westpac forecast US$5,600/oz end-2026 → Massive uplift, becoming Australia's #2 export
- Copper: Westpac forecast US$13,500/t end-2026 → Structural deficit, energy transition demand
- Housing: Forecast 5-8% price growth in 2026, mortgage repayments 51.5% of income
- Population: NOM halving from 518K (2023) to 260K (2026) → Still exceeds housing supply capacity
- Current account: Deficit A$16.6B (Sep Q 2025), terms of trade stable but deteriorating trend

## Cross-Cutting Signals

- RBA hike cycle → Higher mortgage rates → Housing affordability worsens → Consumer confidence weakens → Retail spending risk
- RBA hike → AUD strengthens → Headwind for commodity exporters (AUD-denominated revenues) but supports imports/disinflation
- Iron ore price decline → Fiscal revenue risk (every US$10 fall = A$400M revenue hit) → Wider deficit
- Gold surge → Partially offsets iron ore decline in export earnings → ASX gold miners outperform
- Labour market tight → Wage growth 3.4% → Inconsistent with 2% inflation target → RBA stays hawkish
- Housing supply shortage (200K+ dwellings) → Structural price support even with rate hikes → Shelter inflation sticky
- China trade surplus shrinking (Bloomberg, Feb 2026) → Trade balance deterioration → Current account risk
- Per-capita recession → Political pressure for fiscal stimulus → Deficit widens → Bond supply increases

## Open Tensions

1. **Growth vs inflation:** Economy recovering toward trend but inflation re-accelerating — unusual combination that constrains policy response
2. **Labour market tightness vs consumer weakness:** Unemployment at 4.1% but consumer confidence at 92.9 and per-capita GDP falling — labour market is a lagging indicator
3. **Housing:** Prices rising 5-8% but mortgage stress set to increase with rate hikes — can both persist?
4. **AUD strength vs commodity headwinds:** AUD rallying on rate differentials but China's iron ore diversification and falling prices are medium-term headwinds
5. **RBA forecasting record:** RBA's Nov 2025 forecasts were too low — inflation printed higher than expected. How much credibility do current forecasts deserve?

## FX / Rates / Inflation Implications

- AU 10Y at 4.74% vs US 10Y at 4.09% = 65bp spread → Supports AUD carry
- RBA at 3.85% and potentially rising vs Fed at 3.50-3.75% and stable/easing → Rate differential widens in AUD's favour
- AUD/USD uptrend intact but crowding risk — second-best G10 performer creates momentum/positioning risk
- Inflation peak forecast mid-2026 at 4.2% headline / 3.7% trimmed mean → Painful before it improves
- Real policy rate: 3.85% - 3.4% (trimmed mean) = ~0.45% → Barely restrictive, suggesting more hikes needed if RBA is serious

## Positioning & Sentiment Flags

- AUD is second-best G10 performer YTD — risk of crowded long
- ASX 200 at 20.9x fwd P/E vs 20-year avg 15.9x — 32% premium to history
- Housing: 87% of industry professionals expect price rises → Consensus bullish, vulnerable to disappointment
- Consumer confidence below 100 for extended period → Negative sentiment already priced in
- Gold miners leading ASX performance → Momentum trade, vulnerable to mean-reversion if gold corrects
- RBA rate hike pricing at 76% for May → If inflation surprises down (CPI due 25 Feb), significant unwind potential

# Module: Single Company Analysis

**Depends on:** Relevant thematic/sector module (for context), Macro regime (rates for DCF)
**Feeds into:** Thematic (company as theme expression), Watchlist (add/update)

*Activate when a company deep-dive is requested or when a thematic/sector report flags a name worth investigating. This is a research framework, not a buy/sell recommendation.*

---

## I.1 Business Model & Competitive Position

- What does the company do? How does it make money? Unit economics?
- Competitive position: where is the moat? Porter's Five Forces or equivalent.
- Market share trajectory — gaining or losing? Why?
- Management quality and capital allocation track record

---

## I.2 Financial Profile

- Revenue growth (3-year trend + forward estimates)
- Margin structure (gross, operating, net) and trajectory
- ROIC vs WACC
- Balance sheet: net debt/EBITDA, interest coverage, maturity profile
- FCF generation and conversion
- Capital allocation priorities: reinvestment, buybacks, dividends, M&A

---

## I.3 Valuation (Damodaran-style)

**DCF — Scenario-based:**
Build bear/base/bull with explicit assumptions for revenue growth, margins, reinvestment rate, terminal value. State every assumption. Cost of capital = risk-free rate + ERP + company-specific risk.

**Reverse DCF:**
What growth rate and margins are implied by the current price? Is the market pricing the base case, the bull case, or something else? This is often more informative than the forward DCF.

**Comparable multiples:**
Fwd P/E, EV/EBITDA, P/FCF vs sector peers. Premium/discount justified?

**Key valuation drivers:**
Identify the 2–3 assumptions with the most impact. These are the variables to monitor.

---

## I.4 Thematic Relevance

- What % of revenue/earnings is exposed to the relevant theme?
- Is thematic exposure growing or shrinking?
- How does this company compare to other theme expressions (ETFs, other names, other asset classes)?

---

## I.5 Key Risks

- Rank by probability × impact
- Company-specific risks (execution, regulatory, competitive)
- Sector/macro risks affecting this name disproportionately
- What is the market *not* pricing in?

---

## Output Rules

- Present DCF scenarios as a table with explicit assumptions visible
- **No buy/sell recommendations.** Note whether current valuation prices in base case or something more/less optimistic.
- Always link back to the relevant thesis — why does this company matter for the broader view?

---

## Working Brief Update

After completing, update the brief with:
- Valuation verdict (cheap/fair/expensive relative to what's priced in)
- Key assumption the market is most likely wrong about
- How the company evidence affects the broader theme conviction

# Module: Thematic / Secular Analysis

**Depends on:** Macro regime, relevant asset-class modules for valuation/positioning context
**Feeds into:** Equities (beneficiary mapping, key names), Cross-asset (expression), Scenarios (branching outcomes)

*Activate for themes like AI, cybersecurity, defence spending, energy transition, deglobalisation, demographics, GLP-1, etc.*

---

## G.1 Theme Framework (always include)

- **Theme definition and scope:** What is the investment thesis? What is the structural driver? Key assumptions underpinning the thesis?
- **Assumptions stress-test:** List the 3–5 critical assumptions. For each: how robust is it? What breaks it?
- **Beneficiaries mapping:** Which asset classes, sectors, regions, and instruments benefit?
- **Losers / disrupted:** Who is negatively exposed?
- **Valuation check:** Are beneficiaries already priced for the theme? Decompose: how much is earnings growth vs multiple expansion? Is the multiple justified by growth trajectory or speculative?
- **Timeline and catalysts:** 1-year, 5-year, or 10-year theme? Near-term catalysts that accelerate or derail?
- **Policy and regulatory angle:** Government spending, regulation, subsidies, restrictions
- **Cross-asset expression:** Best way to express the theme — direct equity, ETFs, credit, commodities, FX, or multi-asset basket?
- **Risks to the thesis:** Specific risks particular to this theme, not generic risks.

---

## G.2 Key Names to Watch (include for equity/credit thematics)

Identify **5–10 key stocks or instruments** that are the most direct expressions of the theme. For each:

- Company name + 1–2 sentence thematic relevance
- Market cap tier (mega / large / mid / small)
- Key valuation metric (fwd P/E, EV/EBITDA, or most relevant)
- Revenue/earnings exposure to the theme (% if available)
- Recent earnings momentum (beat/miss, revision direction)
- Specific risk factor for this name

Present as a **summary table**. Frame as "names with high exposure to the theme" — not buy/sell recommendations.

---

## G.3 Earnings Themes (include for equity thematics)

- Key themes from most recent earnings season for companies in this space
- Are companies guiding higher or lower on theme-related revenue/capex?
- What are management teams saying about demand visibility, order pipelines, pricing?
- Divergences between market pricing and what companies are actually reporting?

---

## Secular Horizon Adjustment

For 3–10 year themes: shift emphasis away from current valuations and positioning (noise at this horizon) toward structural drivers — demographics, technology adoption S-curves, policy trajectories, capital cycle dynamics, and multi-year scenario trees. The output format stays the same; the analytical weight shifts.

---

## Working Brief Update

After completing, update the brief with:
- Theme thesis in one sentence
- Strongest assumption and weakest assumption
- Whether beneficiaries are already priced (valuation verdict)
- Cross-cutting: how the theme interacts with macro/rates/FX findings
